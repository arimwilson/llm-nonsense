# Detailed Chapter Outlines

## ch01 - The Offer You Can't Refuse (Or Can You?)
- Opening scene: manager offer call arrives with urgency and social pressure.
- Section 1: why IC success does not predict management fit.
- Section 2: hidden costs (calendar load, emotional labor, decision fatigue).
- Section 3: what to ask before saying yes (scope, support, expectations).
- Section 4: deciding publicly and setting expectations with peers.
- Concrete managerial decision: accept the role only after defining success criteria with the hiring director.
- Reusable tool: Manager Transition Decision Grid.
- Manager actions checklist:
  - list your top three motivations for switching tracks
  - document what you are unwilling to trade away
  - confirm support structure for first 90 days
  - align with your manager on explicit success metrics
- Transition: week one begins before your calendar says it does.

## ch02 - Your First Week Is Already Too Late
- Opening scene: inherited roadmap deadline and unresolved staffing conflict appear in day three.
- Section 1: first-week anti-patterns (announcing solutions too early).
- Section 2: listening sprint (1:1s, team docs, partner interviews).
- Section 3: risk map for commitments and trust gaps.
- Section 4: communicate first-week findings without blame.
- Concrete managerial decision: pause a noncritical launch to reset staffing and reduce hidden risk.
- Reusable tool: First-Week Listening and Risk Map.
- Manager actions checklist:

  - identify top five delivery risks and owners
  - list assumptions that must be validated
  - publish a week-two decision memo
- Transition: the role shift gets personal fast.

## ch09 - The Conversation Nobody Trained You For

- Section 3: running the conversation without overexplaining.
- Section 4: follow-up plan and emotional processing for both sides.
- Explicit Four Bases map in chapter body:
  - Team: impact on collaboration and reliability
  - Role: gap between role expectations and observed behavior

- Transition: performance problems need a framework, not improvisation.
