# Chapter 13: Layoffs, Reorgs, and Other Things That Aren't Your Fault But Are Your Problem

Draft status: scaffold complete.

## Chapter intent
Lead teams through structural change with clarity and credibility.

## Core anecdote
A manager must explain a reorg while many details remain unresolved.

## Key sections
- Known versus unknown communication.
- Team stabilization during uncertainty.
- Rebuilding operating rhythm after change.

## Explicit Four Bases map
- Team: trust and continuity.
- Role: ownership and reporting changes.
- Business: strategic constraints and rationale.
- Legal/Ethical: confidentiality and jurisdictional boundaries.

## Concrete managerial decision
Communicate what is known now and timestamp when unknowns will be updated.

## Manager action checklist
- Separate facts from assumptions.
- Publish update cadence.
- Provide direct support channels.
